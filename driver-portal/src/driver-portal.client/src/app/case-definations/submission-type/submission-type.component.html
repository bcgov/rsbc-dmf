Listed below are the definintions for the various submission types.
  • Diabetic Package – RoadSafetyBC (RSBC) requires more information about your diabetes.
  • Fax-Medical Rationale – A fax requesting medical reasons of support on a recommendation given to RSBC.
  • DMER – Driver's Medical Examination Report- RSBC requires information about the status of any of your medical conditions that may affect driving.
  • Hearing Report - RSBC requires more information to fully assess your hearing.
  • EVF – Examination of Visual Functions- RSBC requires more information to fully assess your vision.
  • Diabetic Driver Questionnaire – is a questionnaire, to be completed by you, to confirm your understanding of your diabetic condition.
  • ERA Results – Enhanced Road Assessment Results- After reviewing information on your Driver Medical Fitness file, RoadSafetyBC (RSBC) requires you to complete an Enhanced Road Assessment (ERA) to determine your fitness and safety to drive.
  • VFT – Visual Field Test- RoadSafetyBC requires more information about your visual fields.
  • Police Report - RoadSafetyBC (RSBC) has received a report from police that raises concerns regarding your medical fitness to drive.
  • PDR – Priority Doctor's Report- RoadSafetyBC (RSBC) has received a medical report that raises concerns regarding your medical fitness to drive.
  • Client Letter In - RoadSafetyBC (RSBC) has received correspondence from you, or from the representative acting on your behalf.
  • Unsolicited Report of Concern– RoadSafetyBC (RSBC) has received a report from a member of the public that raises concerns regarding your medical fitness to drive.
  • Specialist Report – RoadSafetyBC (RSBC) has received information from one or more of your medical practitioners.
  • FDE Report - Functional Driving Evaluation Report-RoadSafetyBC (RSBC) has received the assessment from your Occupational Therapist regarding the outcome of your Functional Driving Evaluation.
  • Diabetic Doctor Report– RoadSafetyBC (RSBC) requires more information about your diabetes. Please have your physician complete Doctor’s Report on a Driver with Diabetes in full and submit it to RSBC.
  • Release of Info Request – RSBC has received a request through FOI.
  • ICBC Re-exam – After reviewing information on your Driver Medical Fitness file, RoadSafetyBC (RSBC) requires you to complete an ICBC Road Test (re-exam) to determine your fitness and safety to drive.
  • Invoice – Billing information submitted for subsidized reviews to RSBC.
  • Notice To Cancel – After reviewing the information on your Driver Medical Fitness file, RSBC has sent a notice to cancel your licence.
  • Letter Out– A letter that RSBC has sent to the client.
  • Restriction Reconsideration – RoadSafetyBC has responded to your request to reconsider a medical fitness decision and/or the application of medical restrictions to your driver’s license.
